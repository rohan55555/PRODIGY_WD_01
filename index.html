Time travel to the future

Time travel to the future is possible, but not in the way you might expect. According to Albert Einstein's theory of relativity, time and motion are relative to each other, and nothing can go faster than the speed of light, which is 186,000 miles per second.0 Time travel happens through what's called "time dilation." While the debate continues over whether travelling into the past is possible, physicists have determined that travelling to the future most certainly is. We can't use a time machine to travel hundreds of years into the past or future, but the math of time travel does affect the things we use every day.1 Global positioning satellites exploit space-time to advance through the years a little faster than the next person.
